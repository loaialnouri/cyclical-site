Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 March 2026

Velo is designed with your privacy in mind. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and your choices.

Data We Access

  • Location: Your GPS position is used to record ride routes, calculate speed and distance, and provide turn-by-turn navigation. Location data is processed on-device and stored locally in your ride history.
  • Health & Fitness: With your permission, Velo reads heart rate data from Apple Watch via HealthKit and writes completed workouts (distance, duration, calories) back to Apple Health. This data never leaves your device except through Apple's own Health infrastructure.
  • Bluetooth Sensors: Velo connects to BLE cycling accessories (heart rate monitors, power meters, cadence sensors, rear radar). Sensor data is processed in real time and stored locally in ride recordings.
  • Motion Data: Accelerometer data may be used to improve speed and elevation accuracy. This data is not stored.

Supported Devices & How Data Is Used

Velo connects to standard Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) cycling accessories. All sensor data is processed on-device in real time and stored locally in your ride recordings.

  • Heart Rate Monitors (BLE Heart Rate Service 0x180D) — Live heart rate is displayed on your dashboard, used to calculate heart rate training zones, and saved with your ride recording. If Apple Health is enabled, heart rate data is also written to HealthKit workouts.
  • Power Meters (BLE Cycling Power Service 0x1818) — Instantaneous power (watts) is displayed in real time, used for training load and intensity calculations, and included in ride recordings and Strava uploads.
  • Speed & Cadence Sensors (BLE Cycling Speed and Cadence Service 0x1816) — Wheel speed and pedalling cadence are displayed on your dashboard and recorded with your ride for post-ride analysis.
  • Rear Radar (Garmin Varia-compatible radar units) — Approaching vehicle alerts are shown in real time during your ride to improve situational awareness. Radar data is not stored.
  • Battery Monitoring (BLE Battery Service 0x180F) — Battery levels of all connected sensors are read and displayed so you know when a device needs charging. Battery data is not stored.

Velo also integrates with:

  • Apple Watch — Live heart rate is read via HealthKit workout sessions when no external heart rate monitor is connected.
  • Apple Health — Completed workouts (distance, duration, calories, heart rate) are saved to Apple Health with your permission.
  • GPX Route Import — You can import route files from any GPX-compatible app or service for turn-by-turn navigation. Routes are stored locally on your device.

Data We Send

  • Elevation API: Route coordinates are sent to the OpenTopoData API (open-source, self-hostable) to fetch elevation profiles. Only latitude/longitude pairs are transmitted — no personal identifiers.
  • Strava (optional): If you choose to connect your Strava account, completed ride data (GPS track, duration, distance) is uploaded to Strava via their official API. This is entirely opt-in and can be disconnected at any time.

Data We Do Not Collect

  • No analytics or crash reporting SDKs
  • No advertising frameworks or ad identifiers
  • No user accounts or registration
  • No server-side storage of your rides or personal data
  • No data sold or shared with third parties

On-Device Storage

All ride history, settings, bike configurations, and cached elevation data are stored locally on your iPhone. Deleting the app removes all data.

Your Choices

  • You can deny or revoke location, Bluetooth, Health, and motion permissions at any time in iOS Settings.
  • Strava integration is optional and can be disconnected.
  • You can delete individual rides or all data by removing the app.

Children

Velo is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

If this policy is updated, the revised version will be posted here with a new date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance.
